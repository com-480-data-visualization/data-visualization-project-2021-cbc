How can we prevent a cardio-vascular disease?

Introduction

Cardio-Vascular diseases are the first cause of death worldwide, the World Health Organisation estimates that they are responsible for about 31% of all global deaths. However, upon preprocessing and data exploration, they seem to only make up 13% of the WHO dataset that we have. It is hard to know why that is for now but we will keep this fact in mind when creating our visualizations so as to not provide incomplete insights. Using our visualizations we’d like to raise awareness towards those diseases. Indeed, we often don’t realize how frequent and diverse the symptoms can be.

We are addressing ourselves to everybody as anybody can be or has a relative that might be more subject to a cardiovascular disease. More awareness on these diseases and common symptoms can help young people be more cautious of their health meanwhile providing at risk people with partial answers and solutions on some conditions they might start to notice. Individual aggraving factors

We want to explore what in your personal way of life, can prevent or on the contrary increase the probability of developping a cardiovascular disease.

How to use

For this first visualization, you can hover over a link to make it light up along with all it's resulting link up to the CVD column. By clinking on a link, the total number of records that corresponds to it appear, along with the percentage it represents with respect with the total number ofr record in the dataset. As people can cumulate multiple factors on the left of the graph, the percentages don't sum up to 100% across factors. Instead, the number on one link represents the total number and proportion of subjects that had the exact combinations of factor-age-CVD. You can easily disable a node by clicking on it, making all the links connected to it dissapear. Finally, you can also rearrange the nodes on their y-axis by drag-and-dropping them where you want on their column.

Influence of time on a cardiovascular disease

Here we want study the evolution of the proportion of cardiovascular related deaths over all deaths worldwide. Though this visualization we want to see whether the proportion of cardiovascular related deaths has increases over the past 30 years. This visualization will also allow us to esily notice the parts of the world more severely touched by cardiovascular diseases and thus help us understand the impact of our environment on the development of such diseases. The user however has to keep in mind that the data recovered from the World Health Organisation is highly dependent on the cooperation of the different states. Indeed as one will notice some countries give data almost every year when others provide very few data.

How to use

Select the desired year with the slider and go over any country you wish to have more information about. It will display the exact percentage of deaths related to cardiovascular diseases for that specific year in the selected country.
